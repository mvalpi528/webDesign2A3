About Us

Surf Seriously is all about teaching you everything you need to know to get started on your surfing journey.

We focus on getting you up to speed on what equipment you're going to need and where you can go to surf. Our site has all the info on the most common board types and even has a custom calculator to help you find the board type that will be perfect for your experience, fitness and skill level.

Once you find the perfect board for you the next thing is to figure out where you want to go surfing? We've got all the info on different types of surf spot and the most popular destinations for when you make your first trip overseas.

The Surfer's Quiver

Shortboard

Short boards are high-performance surfboards, designed to make sharp turns on critical sections of the wave.

Learn More

A high-performance shortboard is a type of surfboard designed to perform critical manoeuvres on steep and hollow waves.

The high-performance shortboards are defined by:

  • narrow outline
  • reduced volume
  • pointed nose
  • pronounced rocker
  • concave bottom contours
  • thin rails
Close

Fish

Fish surfboards are short and wide surf boards that typically have plenty of volume for their size.

Learn More

The fish surfboard is a type of surfboard inspired by the twin-finned boards that gained popularity in the 1970s.

The fish is defined by:

  • shorter length
  • wide outline
  • swallowtail or split tail shape
  • typically has two fins
  • flatter rocker
  • wider nose
Close

Midlength

Midlengths are bigger surfboards that have significantly more volume and can be up to 8ft in length.

Learn More

A midlength surfboard is a versatile and balanced option that bridges the gap between shortboards and longboards.

The midlength is defined by:

  • 6 to 8 feet long
  • rounded nose
  • moderate rocker
  • well-suited for a wide range of conditions
  • relaxed and smooth ride
  • ideal for trimming along the wave's face
